14th August

Pakistan gained independence from British rule on this day in 1947. Let's honor the sacrifices of our forefathers and celebrate our freedom with pride.

About Pakistan

Pakistan is a beautiful country located in South Asia. It was founded on August 14, 1947, as an independent nation for Muslims of the Indian subcontinent. The country shares borders with India, Afghanistan, Iran, and China. Pakistan is known for its rich culture, history, and diverse landscapes, including mountains, deserts, and rivers.

The capital city of Pakistan is Islamabad, while Karachi is the largest city. Pakistan has four provinces: Punjab, Sindh, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, and Balochistan. Each province has its own unique traditions, languages, and cultural heritage. The official language of Pakistan is Urdu, and English is widely used for official purposes.

Pakistan's national flag is green with a white crescent and star. The green color represents the Muslim majority, while the white stripe symbolizes religious minorities. Pakistan is home to several famous landmarks, such as the Badshahi Mosque in Lahore, the ancient city of Mohenjo-Daro, and the scenic Hunza Valley. The people of Pakistan are known for their hospitality and strong sense of community. The country celebrates several national holidays, including Independence Day on August 14 and Pakistan Day on March 23

History of Pakistan

The Partition of India in 1947 led to one of the largest mass migrations in history, with over 15 million people displaced as borders were drawn to create the new nations of India and Pakistan. The division was based on religious lines, resulting in widespread violence and upheaval. The aftermath saw between one to two million deaths as communities were torn apart and millions were forced to flee their ancestral homes to seek refuge across the newly established borders
